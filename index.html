About

I am a Computational Associate at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, working in Cancer Data Science and the Dependency Map Project. My work focuses on computational biology, machine learning, functional genomics, and large language models.

With expertise in both computational methods and biological systems, I develop innovative strategies for discovering new therapeutic targets from genome-scale screens and contribute to open-source machine learning platforms.

I graduated Magna Cum Laude from Dickinson College with a degree in Mathematics and Computer Science, and conducted research on computational analysis of cancer cell lines.

Research Focus

Large Language Models

Applying machine learning and natural language processing techniques to analyze biological data and derive insights from complex genomic information.

Computational Biology

Developing computational methods to analyze biological data, with a focus on cancer genetics and dependencies to identify new therapeutic targets.

Functional Genomics

Studying gene function at a genome-wide scale using CRISPR-Cas9 and RNAi screens to understand genetic dependencies and identify cancer vulnerabilities.
